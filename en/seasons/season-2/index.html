Season II: Awakening of Dragons

Season II is a dragon-themed season content arc. The overall structure is almost identical to Season I, but the core season terms and targets shift from the sea theme to the dragon theme. The combined maximum level of season buildings remains 270, and season rewards and settlement are organized around dragon genes and achievement structure.

Season II Key Summary

If you already played Season I, Season II will feel structurally very familiar.

What to remember first Season II uses almost the same system structure as Season I. That means you can keep the same general flow from the Season I page, while changing only the core terminology to match the dragon theme. Season rewards are linked to dragon genes, and when the season ends, seasonal dragon clans and season skills are reset.

Season II Term Changes

The structure is the same as Season I, but in Season II the core targets and names change to a dragon theme.

Red Line

Gold Flame Dragon Clan

This is the Season II term corresponding to the red ship line in Season I. You can think of it as the more combat-oriented line.

Blue Line

Night Battle Dragon Clan

This is the Season II term corresponding to the blue ship line in Season I. You can think of it as the more growth-focused and stable line.

Summary Season II uses the same progression structure as Season I. So the growth flow, season building management, season resource interpretation, and season content approach explained in Season I can all stay the same, with only the terms replaced to match the Season II dragon theme.

Season Buildings

The season building structure should also be understood the same way as Season I.

Total Max Level 270

Just like in Season I, season buildings reach max level at a combined total of 270. In Season II as well, total combined building level management matters more than isolated building growth.

Growth Priority

Season buildings directly affect early productivity and season progression speed. Rather than pushing only one building too hard, a more balanced upgrade path aligned with the overall season flow is more stable.

On the Season II page, it is cleaner operationally to show only the total season building cap clearly here and move detailed building tables into separate child pages.

Season Rewards

The core season rewards of Season II can be understood around dragon genes and achievement structure.

Basic idea The core objective of the season is to raise dragon resistance and collect dragon genes at the alliance level. The higher these two values go, the more season rewards you can obtain.

Dragon Genes

Dragon genes are produced through city occupation, state battles, and gene contest game modes. Dragon genes directly affect the main reward structure of this season.

Reward Personal Achievements

The higher your dragon resistance becomes, the more season personal achievements you can earn. This is the reward axis players feel first during individual growth.

Alliance Achievement Rewards

By contesting dragon genes for your alliance, you can earn more alliance achievement rewards. As the season moves into the later phase, cumulative alliance contribution matters more than individual progress.

Season Settlement

Dragon genes affect the season settlement rankings of your alliance and your state. In the end, Season II rankings are determined by cumulative contribution and season objective completion.

Season Content

Season II content uses the same framework as Season I, but with a dragon theme applied on top.

1

Early Season Growth

Right after the season starts, the core priority is raising season buildings and key season growth values quickly. Just like in Season I, securing the early production base and season resistance value is most important.

2

Collecting Dragon Genes

The flow of securing dragon genes through city occupation, state battles, and gene contest becomes important. Season rewards and settlement rankings connect directly to this value.

3

Expanding Personal and Alliance Achievements

From the middle of the season onward, alliance-wide cumulative contribution matters more than personal achievements alone. Operating in sync with alliance objectives is more efficient than focusing only on solo growth.

4

Preparing for Season Settlement

In the late season, final settlement ranking becomes important. Dragon gene totals, alliance contribution, and season achievement completion all need to be managed through the end.

Season Reset

You need to clearly understand what remains after the season and what gets reset.

Core sentence After the season ends, once the invasion crisis of the awakened dragons recedes, the dragon clans that helped you leave, and their related attributes no longer apply.

Seasonal Dragon Clans

At the end of the season, the seasonal dragon clans stationed in your settlement depart, and their related attributes no longer apply.

Season Skills

When the next season begins, all progress on acquired season skills is reset. In other words, the accumulated state of season-exclusive skills from Season II does not carry over as-is into the next season.

On the Season II page, it is better to separate season reset into its own section. This is the part players get confused about most often when trying to understand what stays and what disappears after season end.
